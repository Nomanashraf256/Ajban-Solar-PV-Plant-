🚨 FULL EMERGENCY ACTION PLAN | Immediate & Follow-up Actions
STEP 1 · WITNESS

🔔 Perform First Actions

  • Alert Emergency Manager (O&M HSSE Manager) & Lead Site Engineer → state nature, location, injuries.
  • Assess situation, identify dangers → move clear of hazard.
  • If safe → eliminate immediate danger (isolate energy, use extinguisher).
  • Mark area to prevent secondary accidents.
  • Keep calm, protect yourself and coworkers.
  • Notify all affected personnel using alarm or voice.
  • Use Emergency Contact Sheet (posted at access points, offices, warehouse).
📢 Emergency Manager makes walkie-talkie announcement to Emergency Group → all personnel proceed to assembly point.
STEP 2 · INTERNAL ALERT

📡 Alert Internal Contacts & Activate Evacuation

  • Emergency Manager + Lead Site Engineer receive witness report.
  • Announcement via walkie-talkie emergency group → incident details & evacuation order if needed.
  • Emergency Manager gathers Emergency Management Team to assess situation.
  • Site evacuation decision based on severity (chart above).
  • All personnel move to designated assembly points (refer to MP 09 – FORM 3: Evacuation Routes).
  • Head count reported to Emergency Manager.
STEP 3 · EXTERNAL SUPPORT

📞 Call External Emergency Services

  • If escalation is Level 1 or Level 2 → Emergency Manager contacts external authorities: Civil Defense, Police, Ambulance, Hospitals.
  • Use [MP 09 – FORM 2: Emergency Contact Sheet] available at all key locations.
  • For Level 2 → also activate Corporate Crisis Management Team (CMT) for strategic support, media, and resources.
  • Coordinate with external responders: provide site layout, access routes, and incident commander details.
📋 Ensure liaison officer meets authorities at gate.
STEP 4 · POST-INCIDENT

📋 Emergency Debrief & Reporting

  • Debrief Meeting called by Emergency Manager within 48 hours after emergency under control.
  • Location: O&M Contractor’s office. Involve external ER experts if needed.
  • Capture Lessons Learned and update emergency plan quarterly.
  • Incident Report prepared using [MP 01 – Operation HSSE MS Manual] template.
  • If emergency was Level 2 or major crisis → formal Root Cause Analysis (RCA) by trained personnel.
  • Implement corrective actions and update Leg Chart if required.
👥 Key Roles & Communication Protocol

🛡️ Emergency Manager (O&M HSSE Manager)

  • Overall command of emergency response.
  • Determines escalation level (Minor / Level 1 / Level 2) based on chart.
  • Declares evacuation, contacts external agencies, activates CMT if needed.
  • Ensures headcount and coordinates debrief & incident report.

📻 Emergency Group Communication

  • Walkie-talkie network connects all team members instantly.
  • Emergency Manager broadcasts: “Emergency Code, location, assembly point instruction”.
  • All radio traffic focuses on response coordination.
  • Secondary contact via mobile / satellite phone if needed.

📌 Assembly Point Wardens

  • Each assembly point has assigned warden for roll call.
  • Report missing personnel to Emergency Manager immediately.
  • Provide first aid if required and keep evacuees calm.
  • Evacuation routes & maps displayed at strategic points.
